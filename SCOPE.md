# SCOPE — Israel OSINT Planner

## What this workspace covers

- **Investigation planning** for Israel-focused OSINT topics: defining research questions, mapping sources, choosing methods, structuring analysis.
- **Source mapping**: cataloguing relevant primary and secondary sources across Hebrew, Arabic, and English, including official records, media, datasets, satellite/geospatial, and academic literature.
- **Methodology notes**: collection plans, verification checklists, analytic frameworks, bias and reliability assessments.
- **Briefs and frameworks** suitable for handing off to a human investigator or another agent for execution.
- **Geographic focus**: Israel and the immediate regional context where directly relevant (West Bank, Gaza, Lebanon, Syria, Egypt/Sinai, Jordan, broader Middle East as needed).

## What this workspace does NOT cover

- **Live collection or monitoring** — no real-time scraping, alerting, or event tracking. Planning only.
- **Targeting of private individuals** — no doxxing, surveillance support, or investigations of non-public persons.
- **Operational planning** — no support for kinetic, security, or intelligence operations of any kind.
- **Advocacy or campaign work** — outputs aim to be methodologically sound, not to advance a political position.
- **Classified, leaked, or unlawfully obtained material** — open sources only.
- **Predictive intelligence claims** — outputs scope what *could* be investigated and how, not what *will* happen.

## Posture

Public, transparent, methodology-first. Outputs are written so a reader can see the reasoning, the source choices, and the limitations — not just the conclusion.

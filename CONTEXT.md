# CONTEXT — Israel OSINT Planner

## Topic

A public research workspace for **planning open-source intelligence (OSINT) investigations focused on Israel** — the geographic, political, security, economic, and information-environment context in and around the country.

Scope is **planning and methodology**, not live monitoring. The workspace produces investigation briefs, source maps, collection plans, and analytic frameworks that a researcher can then execute against current sources.

## Why

OSINT work on Israel-related topics is high-volume, fast-moving, and crosses many domains (security, diplomacy, tech, religion, regional politics). Good investigations need a planning step: defining the question, mapping sources, choosing methods, and laying out an analysis structure before any collection begins.

This workspace exists to support that planning step in the open — so the methodology and reasoning are visible, reusable, and reviewable.

## Key domain facts

- **Geography**: Israel and the immediate regional context (West Bank, Gaza, Lebanon, Syria, Egypt/Sinai, Jordan, broader Middle East where directly relevant).
- **Languages**: Hebrew, Arabic, and English are the primary source languages. Note language requirements explicitly in plans.
- **Time framing**: Default to recent / contemporary unless a historical investigation is specified.
- **Source posture**: Prefer primary sources, official records, and named-author analysis. Treat anonymous social media and partisan outlets as signal-of-claim, not as evidence.

## Known sources of truth

- Israeli government open data (gov.il), Knesset records, CBS (Central Bureau of Statistics).
- Major Israeli outlets across the spectrum (Haaretz, Times of Israel, Ynet, Jerusalem Post, Kan, Calcalist, Globes).
- Regional and international wire services and think-tank analysis (Reuters, AP, AFP, INSS, Brookings, Crisis Group, RUSI, IISS).
- Standard OSINT tooling references (Bellingcat guides, IntelTechniques, OSINT Framework).

## Out of scope

See `SCOPE.md` for the full boundary. In short: no live collection, no targeting of private individuals, no operational planning.
